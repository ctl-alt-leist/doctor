# Mathematical Foundations

## From Classical to Quantum Fields

The transition from classical to quantum field theory follows a well-established procedure known as canonical quantization. We begin with the classical Lagrangian density and promote fields to operators satisfying specific commutation relations.

### The Scalar Field

Consider a free scalar field described by the Lagrangian density:

$$\mathcal{L} = \frac{1}{2} \left( \partial_\mu \phi \partial^\mu \phi - m^2 \phi^2 \right)$$

The Euler-Lagrange equation yields the Klein-Gordon equation:

$$\left( \Box + m^2 \right) \phi = 0$$

where $\Box = \partial_\mu \partial^\mu$ is the d'Alembert operator.

### Canonical Quantization

Following canonical quantization, we promote $\phi$ and its conjugate momentum $\pi = \partial_\mu \phi$ to operators satisfying equal-time commutation relations:

$$[\hat{\phi}(\mathbf{x}, t), \hat{\pi}(\mathbf{y}, t)] = i \hbar \delta^3(\mathbf{x} - \mathbf{y})$$

$$[\hat{\phi}(\mathbf{x}, t), \hat{\phi}(\mathbf{y}, t)] = [\hat{\pi}(\mathbf{x}, t), \hat{\pi}(\mathbf{y}, t)] = 0$$

These relations ensure the theory respects quantum mechanical principles while maintaining relativistic invariance.

## Mode Expansion and Creation Operators

The general solution to the Klein-Gordon equation can be expanded in plane wave modes:

$$\hat{\phi}(\mathbf{x}, t) = \int \frac{d^3k}{(2\pi)^3} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2E_k}} \left[ \hat{a}(\mathbf{k}) e^{-i E_k t + i \mathbf{k} \cdot \mathbf{x}} + \hat{a}^\dagger(\mathbf{k}) e^{i E_k t - i \mathbf{k} \cdot \mathbf{x}} \right]$$

where $E_k = \sqrt{|\mathbf{k}|^2 + m^2}$ and the operators satisfy:

$$[\hat{a}(\mathbf{k}), \hat{a}^\dagger(\mathbf{k})] = (2\pi)^3 \delta^3(\mathbf{k} - \mathbf{k}')$$

### Physical Interpretation

The operators $\hat{a}^\dagger(\mathbf{k})$ and $\hat{a}(\mathbf{k})$ create and annihilate particles with momentum $\mathbf{k}$, respectively. The vacuum state $|0\rangle$ satisfies:

$$\hat{a}(\mathbf{k}) |0\rangle = 0 \quad \text{for all } \mathbf{k}$$

Multi-particle states are constructed by applying creation operators:

$$|\mathbf{k}_1, \mathbf{k}_2, \ldots, \mathbf{k}_n\rangle = \hat{a}^\dagger(\mathbf{k}_1) \hat{a}^\dagger(\mathbf{k}_2) \cdots \hat{a}^\dagger(\mathbf{k}_n) |0\rangle$$

## Hamiltonian and Energy

The Hamiltonian density corresponding to our Lagrangian is:

$$\mathcal{H} = \frac{1}{2} \left[ \pi^2 + (\nabla \phi)^2 + m^2 \phi^2 \right]$$

In terms of creation and annihilation operators, the total Hamiltonian becomes:

$$\hat{H} = \int \frac{d^3k}{(2\pi)^3} E_k \hat{a}^\dagger(\mathbf{k}) \hat{a}(\mathbf{k}) + E_0$$

where $E_0$ is the infinite zero-point energy that requires careful regularization procedures [@weinberg-1995-a].
