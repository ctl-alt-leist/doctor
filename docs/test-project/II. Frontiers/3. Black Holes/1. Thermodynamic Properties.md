# Thermodynamic Properties

## The Four Laws of Black Hole Mechanics

Black holes exhibit remarkable thermodynamic properties that mirror the classical laws of thermodynamics. This deep connection, first explored by Bekenstein and Hawking, reveals fundamental aspects of quantum gravity.

### The Zeroth Law

The zeroth law states that the surface gravity $\kappa$ is constant over the event horizon of a stationary black hole:

$$\kappa = \text{constant}$$

This is analogous to thermal equilibrium, where temperature is constant throughout a system in equilibrium.

### The First Law

The first law relates changes in the black hole's mass, area, angular momentum, and charge:

$$dM = \frac{\kappa}{8\pi} dA + \Omega dJ + \Phi dQ$$

where:
- $M$ is the mass
- $A$ is the horizon area  
- $J$ is the angular momentum
- $Q$ is the electric charge
- $\Omega$ and $\Phi$ are the angular velocity and electric potential

## Hawking Radiation

Stephen Hawking's groundbreaking discovery showed that black holes emit thermal radiation with temperature:

$$T_H = \frac{\kappa}{2\pi} = \frac{1}{8\pi M}$$

for a Schwarzschild black hole of mass $M$ (in natural units where $G = c = \hbar = k_B = 1$).

### Derivation Outline

The derivation involves analyzing quantum field behavior near the event horizon:

1. **Quantum fields** in curved spacetime undergo mixing of positive and negative frequency modes
2. **Bogoliubov transformations** relate modes in different regions
3. **Thermal spectrum** emerges from entanglement between interior and exterior degrees of freedom

The resulting luminosity follows the Stefan-Boltzmann law:

$$L = \sigma A T^4 = \sigma \cdot 16\pi M^2 \cdot \frac{1}{(8\pi M)^4} = \frac{\sigma}{4\pi M^2}$$

## Bekenstein-Hawking Entropy

The black hole entropy is proportional to the horizon area:

$$S_{BH} = \frac{A}{4G}$$

This area law is remarkable because:

1. **Extensive scaling** - Entropy grows with area, not volume
2. **Maximum entropy** - Black holes have maximum entropy for given mass
3. **Information storage** - Suggests holographic information encoding

### Physical Interpretation

The entropy formula implies that information is encoded on the two-dimensional horizon rather than in the three-dimensional interior. This observation led to the holographic principle, suggesting that all physics in a volume can be described by information on its boundary [@ads-cft-1997-a].

## Page Curve and Information Recovery

The information paradox arises from the apparent conflict between:
- **Quantum mechanics** - Information is preserved in unitary evolution
- **General relativity** - Information falls behind the horizon and is lost

Recent developments suggest the Page curve provides the resolution, showing how information can be recovered through subtle quantum correlations between Hawking radiation and the black hole interior.
