# Two-Dimensional Gravity

Dilaton gravity in two dimensions strips the black-hole information problem down
to a solvable core. The Callan-Giddings-Harvey-Strominger model is the canonical
example, with action:

$$
S = \frac{1}{2π} \int d^2x \, \sqrt{-g} \, e^{-2φ} \left[
   R + 4 (∇φ)^2 + 4 λ^2
\right]
$$

where $φ$ is the dilaton and $λ$ sets the cosmological scale.

## The Classical Solution

The linear-dilaton vacuum solves the equations of motion, and a shock wave of
matter collapses it into a black hole whose horizon is visible directly in the
conformal gauge.

## Why the Toy Model Helps

Because the theory is exactly solvable at the semiclassical level, the Page
curve can be computed rather than assumed. This is what makes the two-dimensional
setting the natural first laboratory for the paradox proper.
