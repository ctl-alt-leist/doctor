# Useful Integrals

The Gaussian integral underlies every perturbative calculation in the main text:

$$
\int_{-∞}^{∞} dx \, e^{-a x^2} = \sqrt{\frac{π}{a}}
$$

Its moments follow by differentiation with respect to $a$.

## The Path-Integral Generalization

Promoting the single variable to a field and the coefficient to an operator
gives the free-field generating functional:

$$
Z_0[J] = \exp\left[
   \frac{1}{2} \int d^4x \, d^4y \, J(x) \, G(x, y) \, J(y)
\right]
$$

where $G(x, y)$ is the Feynman propagator.
