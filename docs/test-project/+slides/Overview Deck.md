## The Central Objects

Fields, not particles, are fundamental. A particle is an excitation of a field.

- Canonical quantization promotes fields to operators
- The propagator encodes how excitations move
- Interactions are vertices where propagators meet

---

## The Free Scalar

The equation of motion for a free scalar field is:

$$
(\partial_\mu \partial^\mu + m^2) \phi = 0
$$

and its canonical commutator is $[\phi(x), \pi(y)] = i \hbar \delta^3(x - y)$.

---

## Black Holes Enter

A black hole radiates at the Hawking temperature:

$$
T_H = \frac{\hbar c^3}{8 \pi G M k_B}
$$

The information paradox is what happens to correlations as $M \to 0$.

---

## Summary

- Fields are primary; particles are excitations
- Propagators and vertices build every amplitude
- Black-hole thermodynamics ties gravity to information
